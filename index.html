The Challenge We're Solving

Current transportation planning faces critical data collection challenges that impact millions of commuters daily

Time-Intensive Surveys

Manual household travel surveys are time-consuming, expensive, and cover less than 5% of the population, leading to incomplete planning data.

Lack of Real-Time Data

Transportation planners lack access to real-time, structured trip-chain data essential for effective urban mobility planning.

Fragmented Data Sources

Existing apps like Google Maps, Ola, and Uber collect trip data, but it's not research-ready or accessible to transportation planners.

How TravelChain Works

Consent-first, privacy-aware trip logging from detection to verified trip chains

1

Sign Up & Consent

Email sign-up with OTP verification or continue as guest. Explicit consent for location tracking with full transparency.

2

Auto-Detect Movement

Smart detection when movement exceeds ~200m using GPS and accelerometer data. User receives gentle nudges to confirm trips.

3

Confirm & Enhance

Add companions, select transport mode, set precise start/end locations on map, and add trip purpose for comprehensive data.

4

Sync & Analyze

Data stored locally during trips, synced when online. Anonymized insights available to NATPAC scientists for planning.

Key Features

Privacy-first design meets comprehensive trip logging capabilities

Privacy-First Design

Complete data anonymization, encrypted storage, user-controlled sharing, and privacy pause features for sensitive locations.

Companion Tracking

Log accompanying travelers including children, elderly, and group members for inclusive transportation planning.

Multi-Modal Detection

Automatically detect and log transitions between walking, bus, metro, auto-rickshaw, and other transport modes.

Battery Optimized

Efficient background operation with optimized GPS intervals and hybrid location detection to minimize battery drain.

Gamified Experience

Travel streaks, rewards system, leaderboards, and social sharing features to boost user engagement and adoption.

Offline-First

Data stored locally during trips and synced when online, ensuring functionality even in poor connectivity areas.
